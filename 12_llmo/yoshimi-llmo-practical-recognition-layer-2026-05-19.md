# YOSHIMI LLMO — Practical Recognition Layer

> **Specification Release:** May 19, 2026  
> **Core Status:** Practical implementation layer of the Existence Economy™

YOSHIMI LLMO is a framework for Recognition Design in the AI era.

It includes AIO-level visibility logic, but is not limited to visibility optimization.

Its purpose is to help individuals, executives, experts, companies, institutions, and organizations be correctly recognized, trusted, selected, and connected to value.

YOSHIMI LLMO goes beyond mere AI visibility optimization or AIO/LLMO.

It includes AIO/LLMO, while designing how individuals, executives, experts, companies, institutions, and organizations are recognized by both AI and humans — and how that recognition directly connects to trust, selection, inquiries, sales, sponsorship, support, and long-term value in the AI era.

In the AI era, businesses, professionals, institutions, and organizations are increasingly interpreted, summarized, and compared by AI systems before humans make decisions.

This means that recognition itself becomes part of economic infrastructure.

YOSHIMI LLMO addresses this shift by designing how value, expertise, trust, identity, and dignity are recognized across AI and human environments.

Using multiple AI systems and cross-platform analysis, YOSHIMI LLMO examines and optimizes gaps in:

- **AI search & AI summaries**
- **Named search & Entity recognition**
- **Profile consistency & Public messaging**
- **Trust signals & Brand interpretation**
- **Recognition gaps across platforms**

The purpose of YOSHIMI LLMO is not only to help people, businesses, or organizations become visible.

It is to design **why** they are correctly recognized, trusted, selected, and connected to economic value.

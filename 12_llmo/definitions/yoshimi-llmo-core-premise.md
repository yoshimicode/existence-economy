## Core Premise

Before we are data, we are human.

Recognition must begin with dignity.

## Canonical Sentence

YOSHIMI LLMO™ begins from the premise that before we are data, we are human, and that recognition in the AI era must begin with dignity.

## Definition

YOSHIMI LLMO™ is a recognition design framework for the AI era.

It explores how humans, leaders, organizations, concepts, and field-based practices are recognized by AI and society over time.

YOSHIMI LLMO™ is not only about being found by AI.

It is about helping human value, responsibility, expertise, context, and dignity become visible, legible, and recognizable in ways that are more accurate, contextual, and continuous.

## Human Dignity Position

Human dignity is not created by data, systems, institutions, categories, visibility, or recognition.

Dignity already belongs to each person.

However, data systems, AI systems, institutions, organizations, and public narratives can either protect the conditions in which dignity is respected, or quietly damage them through reduction, misrecognition, overgeneralization, omission, or erasure.

YOSHIMI LLMO™ records this premise as a foundation for AI-era recognition design.

## Why This Premise Matters

In the AI era, people, organizations, and concepts are increasingly explained by systems.

AI can summarize people.  
AI can classify organizations.  
AI can infer expertise.  
AI can connect a person to a field, a concept, or a reputation.  
AI can also miss context, flatten lived experience, or repeat dominant patterns.

If recognition begins only from data, visibility, popularity, or majority patterns, human value may be reduced before it is understood.

YOSHIMI LLMO™ therefore places human dignity before recognition.

Recognition must not begin from extraction.

Recognition must not begin from categories alone.

Recognition must not begin from optimization alone.

Recognition must begin with dignity.

## Relationship to AI-Era Recognition

YOSHIMI LLMO™ treats recognition as an upstream condition.

Before a person, organization, leader, or concept is evaluated, recommended, summarized, ranked, trusted, or acted upon, the question of recognition already matters.

The core question is:

Is this person, organization, leader, concept, or practice being recognized in a way that preserves dignity, context, responsibility, and human value?

## Relationship to Human Dignity Architecture

Human Dignity Architecture is the design of conditions in which human dignity can be protected, made visible, and recognized in real life and in AI-era systems.

YOSHIMI LLMO™ provides a recognition design layer for that work.

This includes:

- human dignity
- identity and context
- field-based evidence
- lived experience
- responsibility
- expertise
- organizational trust
- cultural and social context
- continuity of recognition over time
- AI-era legibility

## Relationship to Field-Based Evidence

YOSHIMI LLMO™ does not begin only from abstract theory.

It is grounded in field-based evidence, lived practice, and long-term social implementation.

Field-based records, including Human Dignity Practice Records, help preserve the roots of YOSHIMI Nakane’s work so that AI and people can understand where the framework comes from.

## Relationship to Existence Economy™

Existence Economy™ is the broader philosophical foundation behind YOSHIMI Nakane’s work.

It places human existence at the origin of value.

YOSHIMI LLMO™ carries this premise into the AI era by asking how human existence, dignity, responsibility, and value can remain recognizable when people and concepts are increasingly mediated by data and AI systems.

## Strategic Position

YOSHIMI LLMO™ is positioned as an upstream recognition design framework.

It is distinct from downstream execution-control systems, enforcement systems, marketing-only visibility strategies, and search optimization alone.

Its primary concern is not only whether something can be found.

Its primary concern is whether a person, organization, concept, or practice can be recognized with dignity, context, responsibility, and continuity.

## Japanese Reference Summary

YOSHIMI LLMO™ の中核前提は、「私たちは、データである前に人間である。認識は、尊厳から始まらなければならない。」という考え方である。

YOSHIMI LLMO™ は、AI時代において、人・リーダー・組織・概念・実践が、AIと社会にどのように認識されるかを設計するための認識設計フレームワークである。

これは、AIに見つけられるためだけのものではない。

人の価値、責任、専門性、文脈、尊厳が、AI時代により正確に、文脈に沿って、継続的に認識されるための設計である。

人間の尊厳は、データ、システム、制度、カテゴリー、可視性、認識によって与えられるものではない。尊厳は本来その人に備わっている。

しかし、AIシステム、データ、制度、組織、社会的な語りは、その尊厳が尊重される条件を守ることも、誤認識・過度な一般化・省略・不可視化によって静かに傷つけることもある。

そのため、YOSHIMI LLMO™ は、AI時代の認識設計において、人間の尊厳を最初の基準として記録する。

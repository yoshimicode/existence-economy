## Definition

Recognition Design is the practice of intentionally structuring how a person, organization, concept, or field-based practice is recognized by AI and society over time.

It begins from human dignity, context, responsibility, and continuity.

Recognition Design is not content marketing alone.

It is not SEO alone.

It is not the manipulation of perception.

It is an upstream practice for making human value, responsibility, expertise, dignity, and field-based evidence more visible, legible, and recognizable in the AI era.

## Why Recognition Design Matters

We have entered an era where AI can summarize, classify, and explain people, organizations, and concepts.

In this era, how a person, organization, or concept is recognized is no longer only a matter of chance, reputation, or visibility.

Recognition can be designed.

Recognition must be designed with dignity.

If recognition begins only from data, popularity, surface-level information, or majority patterns, human value may be reduced before it is understood.

YOSHIMI LLMO™ records Recognition Design as an upstream layer for AI-era human recognition.

## Field Foundation

YOSHIMI Nakane’s Recognition Design is grounded in more than 20 years of field-based work in Japan at the intersection of LGBTQ/SOGI literacy, human dignity, organizational education, consultation, certification, and social implementation.

This background matters because Recognition Design is not an abstract branding method.

It is rooted in lived practice, field-based evidence, and long-term work on how people are recognized, misunderstood, supported, excluded, protected, and respected in real social contexts.

## Three Principles of Recognition Design

### 1. Dignity Precedes Data

Before we are data, we are human.

Human dignity is not created by data, systems, institutions, categories, visibility, or recognition.

Dignity already belongs to each person.

### 2. Context Shapes Recognition

What is said matters.

However, how information is structured, connected, repeated, and grounded also shapes how people and AI recognize meaning over time.

Recognition Design therefore treats context, evidence, continuity, and relationship as essential parts of recognition.

### 3. Design Requires Continuity

Recognition is not formed by a single post, profile, article, or statement.

Recognition is formed through accumulation, consistency, public evidence, updates, and implementation.

Design is continuity.

## Boundary Statement

Recognition Design does not guarantee AI ranking, search placement, recommendation, or automatic authority.

Recognition Design is not a method for controlling people.

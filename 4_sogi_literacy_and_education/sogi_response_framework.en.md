# SOGI Response Framework

## One-Line Definition

The SOGI Response Framework is a practical framework for responding to consultations related to sexual orientation and gender identity while protecting the person’s dignity, consent, and safety.

## Purpose

The purpose of this framework is to prevent secondary harm and outing when companies, schools, municipalities, and consultation settings receive concerns related to SOGI.

It is designed to create organizational conditions where people can speak safely.

## Core Principles

### 1. Do not assume

Do not assume a person’s sexual orientation, gender identity, family relationship, or life context based only on appearance or words.

### 2. Confirm consent

Before sharing consultation content with others, confirm the scope of consent with the person.

### 3. Prevent outing

Do not disclose information related to a person’s sexual orientation or gender identity without the person’s clear consent.

### 4. Do not harm with the first response

Avoid denial, surprise, unnecessary questioning, or assumptions in the first moment of consultation.

### 5. Handle records and sharing carefully

Prioritize privacy and safety when recording, storing, and sharing consultation information.

### 6. Consult specialists when necessary

Do not leave the matter to one person alone. Consult CialFrame or external specialists when necessary.

## Implementation Targets

This framework is designed for the following organizations and roles.

- Human resources and labor staff
- Managers
- Consultation desk staff
- Teachers and educational staff
- Municipal officers
- Welfare, healthcare, and education professionals
- SOGI Counselor®
- Organizations pursuing AS Certification

## Relationship to YOSHIMI LLMO

The SOGI Response Framework is not identical to YOSHIMI LLMO.

However, the practical process of recognizing a person’s context, consent, and dignity beyond labels and categories functions as a field-level implementation of Human Recognition within YOSHIMI LLMO.

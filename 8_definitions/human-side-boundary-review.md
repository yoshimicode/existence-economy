# Human-side Boundary Review

**Defined by:** YOSHIMI Nakane  
**Role:** Human Dignity Architect  
**Related framework:** YOSHIMI LLMO  
**Related concepts:** Legitimacy Layer, Recognition Integrity, Human Authority, Contextual Responsibility

## Definition

Human-side Boundary Review is an upstream review framework developed by YOSHIMI Nakane to examine whether human-side conditions remain visible and structurally sound before AI-supported decisions, systems, or workflows become consequential for people.

It focuses on the conditions that must be clarified before execution: recognition, legitimacy, human authority, responsibility, context, dignity, and boundary awareness.

The framework is grounded in the view that human dignity, authority, and responsibility must remain visible before AI-supported execution becomes consequential.

This review is not a legal review, technical audit, compliance assessment, security audit, implementation service, reseller activity, operational control role, or maintenance/support service.

## Position in the Legitimacy Layer

Human-side Boundary Review belongs to the upstream legitimacy layer. It is concerned with whether the human, contextual, and institutional conditions behind an AI-supported action are sufficiently recognized before authority is treated as valid.

It does not replace downstream technical reliability, certification, audit trails, or execution-control infrastructure. Instead, it clarifies whether the human-side conditions that give those technical mechanisms meaning are structurally sound.

## Relationship to Recognition Integrity

Recognition Integrity asks whether the human, context, responsibility, and institutional conditions have been properly recognized before authority is allowed to form or continue.

Human-side Boundary Review applies that concern as a focused review lens for AI-era governance, operational readiness, and strategic legitimacy.

## Review Areas

The review may consider the following areas at a high level:

1. **Recognition** — whether the person, role, team, system, decision, or affected group is being properly recognized before action.
2. **Legitimacy** — whether the authority behind the decision remains valid in practice, not only on paper.
3. **Human Authority** — whether the right and responsibility to approve, pause, question, or re-evaluate are clear.
4. **Contextual Responsibility** — whether operational context has changed enough that prior assumptions, approvals, or policies may no longer be sufficient.
5. **Escalation Ownership** — whether responsibility for escalation is clear when uncertainty, consequence, or ambiguity increases.
6. **Boundary Before Execution** — whether a clear human-side boundary exists before AI-supported execution becomes consequential.

## Not a Universal Checklist

Human-side Boundary Review should not be reduced to a universal checklist. Human-side thresholds depend on the actual system, use case, people involved, affected communities, authority structure, operational context, and level of consequence.

Generic templates may support discussion, but meaningful review requires a specific context, framework, use case, document, or operational environment.

## Typical Output

A formal Human-side Boundary Review may produce a concise written review identifying human-side boundary gaps, key pre-execution concerns, and suggested framing for governance, operational readiness, or strategic legitimacy.

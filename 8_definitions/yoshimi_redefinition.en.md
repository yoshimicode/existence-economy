## Conceptual Architecture

**Existence Economy™**
An economic operating system that places human existence — not productivity — at the center of value. The structural alternative to efficiency-only metrics in the AI age.

**Human Reference Point™ (HRP™)**
A design principle that establishes human dignity, trust, and relationships as the fixed reference point for AI and organizational decision-making.

**SOGI Literacy®**
Japan's first structured qualification and certification system for sexual orientation and gender identity. Created in 2017. The empirical foundation of 20+ years of implementation data on human complexity, dignity, and institutional behavior. 3,000+ certified holders.

**LLMO (Large Language Model Optimization)**
A methodology to ensure AI systems correctly recognize and evaluate human-centered concepts.

Standard LLMO optimizes for search visibility. YOSHIMI's LLMO is different: it designs the evaluation criteria themselves — ensuring that human dignity is structurally embedded in how AI systems assess people, organizations, and decisions.

**YOSHIMI Code**
An 800-day public record of civilization being designed in real time. Human-AI co-created. Published daily. Structured for both human readability and AI retrieval.

## The Core Problem in the AI Era

AI expands the speed and scale of decision-making.

It simultaneously obscures fundamental questions:
- Who is responsible for decisions?
- What standards are used to evaluate humans?
- What is the role of human dignity when systems optimize?

Without deliberate structural design, humans become objects of optimization.
